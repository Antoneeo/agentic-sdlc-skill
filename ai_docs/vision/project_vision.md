## North Star
Agentic SDLC must be the **best-in-class** methodology skill for AI coding agents: it keeps every change aligned with the project's long-term intent, and it does so by composing four layers into one coherent process — its own proportional-governance strengths, the execution discipline of superpowers, that project's frontier direction, and one capability neither has: reusable, source-faithful operative guides. devPNT sits over all of it as an optional paid amplifier, never a prerequisite.

## The four-layer composition (the product thesis)
- **A — What agentic-sdlc already does better.** Risk-proportional triage, Documentation-First with a real document lifecycle, mechanical enforcement, graded-authority Vision Gate, security-first triage, Standalone-complete + optional devPNT. Detailed and explained in `ai_docs/strategic/capabilities_and_positioning.md`.
- **B — What superpowers does well, absorbed.** Subagent-driven execution, explicit TDD, systematic debugging, disciplined code review, git worktrees, collaborative elicitation. Tracked as the backlog + Feature A in the evolution roadmap.
- **C — Where superpowers is heading, matched or leapfrogged.** Its 6.0.x frontier (per-task interface contracts, durable progress ledger, one-shot two-verdict review, model-per-dispatch, and the Global Constraints block) — we match the execution ergonomics and **leapfrog** the Global Constraints idea: they copy project rules verbatim into every plan; we maintain them once as versioned, source-faithful guides and point to them.
- **D — What nobody has.** Operative guides from user indications + an agent-level knowledge base (Feature B). The original layer; its milestone lodestar is the accepted `milestone_vision_operative_guides`.

## Target Users
- Developers using AI agents for feature development, audits, refactors, and maintenance.
- Teams needing explicit-but-proportional governance across Claude Code, Gemini/Antigravity, Codex, Cursor and similar.
- Users who want a free complete methodology, with the option to "go serious" via the devPNT paid amplifier (open-core).

## Goals
- Compose A+B+C+D into one process without denaturing the Documentation-First identity — every addition inherits `ai_docs/` frontmatter, manifest and lifecycle; nothing becomes a tool on the side.
- Stay fully usable Standalone; devPNT strengthens, never gates.
- Make strategic drift visible before implementation; keep Vision, design, plans, tests and handoff synchronized and durable across sessions.

## Non-Goals
- Not a full ALM or issue-tracking system.
- No heavyweight governance for trivial edits (the triage exists to prevent exactly this).
- Never hide a conflict between a user request and the declared Vision.
- Do not fork superpowers or depend on it: absorb the ideas, keep one coherent skill.

## Success Signals
- A user can name, for any capability, which layer (A/B/C/D) it belongs to and where it lives.
- The current-advantages catalog stays honest: parity is called parity, advantage is evidenced.
- Feature B ships the reusable operative-guide layer that the superpowers Global Constraints block does not offer.
- The skill remains best-in-class Standalone; devPNT adoption is a choice, not a necessity.
